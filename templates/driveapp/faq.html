General Questions

What is GDrive Transfer?

GDrive Transfer is a secure web application that allows you to transfer files and folders between different Google Drive accounts. Whether you're switching to a new personal account or migrating to a business account, our service makes it easy to move your data safely and efficiently.

Is GDrive Transfer free to use?

Yes! GDrive Transfer is completely free to use. There are no hidden fees, premium features, or subscription costs. We provide full functionality to all users at no charge.

How many files can I transfer?

There's no limit on the number of files you can transfer. You can transfer individual files, entire folders, or your complete Google Drive. The only limitation is the available storage space in your destination Google Drive account.

Security & Privacy

Is my data safe during the transfer?

Absolutely! Your files are transferred directly between your Google accounts using secure OAuth authentication. We never store, access, or view your files during the transfer process. All data transmission is encrypted and follows industry-standard security practices.

What permissions do you need to access my Google Drive?

We request only the minimum permissions necessary: read access to your source Google Drive (to list and copy files) and write access to your destination Google Drive (to create folders and copy files). We cannot read your emails, access other Google services, or modify files in your source drive.

Do you store my files or Google account information?

No, we never store your files or personal Google account information. Files are transferred directly between your accounts, and authentication tokens are only kept temporarily in your browser session for the duration of the transfer.

Can I revoke access after the transfer is complete?

Yes! You can revoke GDrive Transfer's access to your Google accounts at any time through your Google Account settings or through our privacy dashboard. We recommend doing this after your transfers are complete for additional security.

Transfer Process

How long does a transfer take?

Transfer speed depends on several factors: the number and size of files, your internet connection, and Google's server load. Small folders (under 100 files) typically complete in minutes, while large archives with thousands of files may take several hours. You can monitor progress in real-time on the transfer status page.

What happens if my transfer fails or gets interrupted?

If a transfer fails or gets interrupted, you can simply restart it. Our system will skip files that were already successfully transferred, so you won't get duplicates. Check the transfer logs to see which files may need to be retried.

Are folder structures preserved during transfer?

Yes! GDrive Transfer maintains your exact folder hierarchy and file organization. All folders, subfolders, and file locations will be preserved exactly as they were in your source account.

Can I cancel a transfer that's in progress?

Yes, you can cancel any active transfer from the transfer status page. Files that have already been transferred will remain in your destination drive, but the process will stop immediately.

Technical Questions

What file types are supported?

GDrive Transfer supports all file types that Google Drive supports, including documents, images, videos, archives, and Google Workspace files (Docs, Sheets, Slides, etc.). There are no restrictions on file types or formats.

Can I transfer shared files or files shared with me?

You can only transfer files that you own or have edit access to. Files that are simply shared with you (view-only) cannot be transferred due to Google Drive's permissions system. However, files you own that are shared with others can be transferred.

Do sharing permissions transfer with the files?

No, sharing permissions do not transfer. All transferred files will be owned by the destination account and will not have any sharing permissions. You'll need to reshare files manually if needed.

What browsers are supported?

GDrive Transfer works on all modern browsers including Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge. We recommend using the latest version of your browser for the best experience.

Troubleshooting

I'm getting an authentication error. What should I do?

Authentication errors usually occur when you're signed into multiple Google accounts. Try signing out of all Google accounts in your browser, then sign back in with just the account you want to use. Make sure you're using the correct account for each step of the transfer process.

My transfer is stuck or moving very slowly. What can I do?

Slow transfers can be caused by large files, network issues, or high server load. Try refreshing the status page to see if progress updates. If the transfer appears stuck for more than 30 minutes, you can cancel it and restart with a smaller batch of files.

Some files didn't transfer. Why?

Files may not transfer due to permission issues, file corruption, or Google Drive API limits. Check the transfer logs for specific error messages. Common issues include files that are too large, files with special characters in names, or temporary Google API errors.
